# Abstract and Highlights

## Bibliographic Information

- **Title:** The limits of Timoshenko beam theory applied to impact problems of layered beams
- **Author:** Vítězslav Adámek (NTIS, University of West Bohemia, Pilsen, Czech Republic)
- **Journal:** International Journal of Mechanical Sciences (2018)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ijmecsci.2018.07.001
- **Received:** 22 April 2018 | **Revised:** 13 June 2018 | **Accepted:** 2 July 2018

## Abstract

The aim of this article is to demonstrate and discuss the possibilities of the application of classical Timoshenko beam theory to problems of three-layered elastic beams with symmetric composition. In particular, the equivalent single-layer first-order shear deformation theory (FSDT) with the variation of Timoshenko shear coefficient is applied.

Three common types of beams are chosen for this study:

- **(i) B1** — a beam composed of two stiff thick plies bonded by a thin soft layer
- **(ii) B2** — a soft-core sandwich beam
- **(iii) B3** — a beam of sandwich-like composition having a core stiffer than the face sheets

The possibilities of the application of FSDT to these basic types and their modifications are studied on their transient responses to a pulse of impact character. Comparing FSDT results with results of the exact two-dimensional theory under the assumption of plane stress, the limits and precision of FSDT are discussed.

**Key finding:** FSDT can give very precise results in case (iii), quite good results in case (i), and questionable results for soft-core sandwiches (ii).

**Keywords:** layered beam, sandwich beam, Timoshenko beam, impact, analytical solution, wave propagation

## Highlights

- Solution for impact problem of a symmetric three-layered beam derived based on FSDT.
- Exact 2D solution for impact problem of an arbitrary three-layered beam derived.
- Single-layer FSDT is precise when layers have similar stiffness and wave velocities.
- Single-layer FSDT used to soft-core beams under impact gives estimate of deflection only.
- Single-layer FSDT can be applied to impact problem of beams with a thin soft layer.
