## Introduction

### Background

Polymeric-matrix fibre composites have become an important engineering material family, providing the opportunity to tailor material properties by careful choice of fibre, matrix, and manufacturing method. Glass-reinforced polyester resin is among the most common, and filament winding produces directional composites with high fibre content and advantageous strength-to-weight ratios.

### The Filament Winding Process

In filament winding, a continuous glass-fibre roving is impregnated with liquid resin and wound under tension onto a mandrel (former). The fibre must follow a **geodesic path** on the mandrel surface to avoid slipping. After complete coverage, the resin is cured to produce a laminated composite.

- Computer Numerical Control (CNC) machines now allow complex, non-axisymmetric components to be wound
- The CAD/CAM software CADFIL (developed at the University of Nottingham) generates CNC part-programs from theoretically computed fibre paths

### The Problem: FE Modelling of Filament-Wound Components

Filament-wound composites are:
- **Orthotropic**: properties differ in three mutually perpendicular planes
- **Inhomogeneous**: thickness, lamination sequence, and ply directions vary continuously over the mandrel surface
- **Complex**: the alternating lamination sequence created by successive winding passes adds further structural complexity

Classical analytical methods are limited to simple geometries (spheres, cylinders). For complex shapes, finite element analysis is essential—but the volume of input data required (material directions, thicknesses, lamination sequences) is enormous and cannot be generated by standard mesh generation packages.

### Thesis Objectives

1. Develop algorithms for automatic FE model generation for filament-wound structures based on CADFIL CAD/CAM data
2. Encode these algorithms as computer programs (FILFEM I for axisymmetric, FILFEM II for non-axisymmetric components)
3. Characterise the elastic properties of typical filament-wound GRP experimentally
4. Validate the model generation systems against experiments on manufactured components

### Key Systems Developed

| System | Scope | Status |
|--------|-------|--------|
| FILFEM I | Axisymmetric components (CADFIL I input) | Fully developed and tested |
| FILFEM II | Non-axisymmetric components (CADFIL II input) | Basic operation established |

The link between fibre path data and FE model generation is the principal contribution of this thesis.
