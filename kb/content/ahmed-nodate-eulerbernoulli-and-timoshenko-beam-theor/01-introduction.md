# Introduction

## Structural Significance of Beam Theory

Most practical engineering structures consist of structural members such as beams, plates, and shells. Beam members are characterized by having a large ratio of length to lateral dimensions and are subjected to forces both along and transverse to their length, as well as moments.

The corresponding unknowns in beam problems depend only on the beam-axis position, and the structural behavior is governed by well-known beam theories, which represent the cornerstone of more modern analysis theories for many other structural members.

## Classification of Beam Theories

Beam theories for small deflections are classified into two main groups:

1. **Euler-Bernoulli Beam Theory (EBBT)**: Ignores shear deformations
2. **Timoshenko Beam Theory (TBT)**: Accounts for shear deformation effects

## Paper Objectives

This comprehensive revision presents:
- Detailed fundamentals of both Euler-Bernoulli and Timoshenko beam theories
- Analytical solutions to classic beam problems
- Numerical finite element solutions using mixed formulation
- Applications and illustrative examples comparing both theories

## Importance for Advanced Topics

Understanding these foundational beam theories is essential for:
- Formulating finite elements of thin and thick structural members
- Plate and shell analysis (where beams are structural counterparts)
- Development of modern computational methods
- Accurate prediction of structural response across various loading conditions
