# Abstract and Introduction

## Abstract

A novel and simplified torque and drag (T&D) model was developed to predict T&D values while drilling both curve and tangent sections of high-angle wells. The model is based on a soft-string concept in which the drill string (D/S) is assumed to be a chain lying on the lower side of the well capable of transmitting torsional forces. Key differentiators include accounting for viscous drilling fluid torque and friction losses within the top drive system (TDS), which are typically ignored in commercial packages.

The model was validated against field data from four wells in the Western Desert, Egypt. Results show negligible error for drag calculations and approximately 10% APRD for torque calculations.

## Introduction

High-angle, horizontal, and extended-reach wells (ERWs) are increasingly common in the petroleum industry. Excessive T&D is one of the major limitations of such wells. Torque is the rotational force needed to overcome resistance from geometric, bit, drive system, and viscous sources. Drag is the axial force opposing string movement; it is higher during tripping without rotation and reduces when the string rotates.

### Hazards of Unmanaged T&D

Failure to control T&D can result in:
- Parting of the D/S or BHA
- Reduction of true weight on bit (WOB) and rate of penetration
- Sinusoidal or helical buckling of the D/S
- Difficulty steering and orienting downhole tools
- Wear of equipment, tubulars, and casing
- Difficulty running lower completions in horizontal wells
- Impaired jar effectiveness during fishing operations

### Soft-String vs. Stiff-String Models

Two broad T&D model categories exist:

- **Soft-string model**: Assumes continuous contact between D/S and wellbore with a smooth path between survey stations. Simple mathematics; preferred for field use. Less accurate at high dog-leg severity (DLS) or with stiff BHA components.
- **Stiff-string model**: Assumes point contact; requires closely spaced survey stations and complex mathematics. No single industry-standard version exists. A DLS threshold of 30°/100 ft is sometimes used to decide between them.

The first mathematical T&D model was developed by Johancsik et al. (1984) using the soft-string approach. Sheppard et al. (1987) later refined this into the backbone of current industry models.

### Overlooked Torque Components

Most current T&D packages ignore:
1. **Viscous torque** — caused by rotation of the D/S against drilling fluid; can be quantified by analogy with viscometer measurements.
2. **TDS friction losses** — in old or poorly maintained top drives, internal friction can account for up to 50% of the total surface torque reading.
3. **Bit torque (TOB)** — dependent on bit aggressiveness and WOB.

Understanding TDS torque losses is critical for correct interpretation of surface torque gauges and for safe drilling within D/S and rig torque limits.
