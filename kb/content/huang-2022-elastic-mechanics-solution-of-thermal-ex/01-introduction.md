## Overview of Thermal Expansion Materials

### Material Classification

The coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE) is a critical indicator of thermophysical properties used to characterize geometric variations caused by temperature changes. Materials are classified into three categories:

1. **Positive Thermal Expansion (PTE) Materials**: Most natural materials expand when heated and contract when cooled
2. **Micro-Thermal Expansion Materials**: Materials with extremely small CTEs
3. **Negative Thermal Expansion (NTE) Materials**: Materials with negative CTEs that contract when heated

### Natural vs. Engineered NTE Materials

Natural NTE materials (e.g., certain zeolites) exhibit several limitations:
- NTE effect observable only in narrow temperature ranges (extremely low or high)
- Significant anisotropy
- Low mechanical strength
- Unsuitable for most engineering applications

This has led to research focusing on **engineered NTE metamaterials** composed of two or more PTE materials, where CTEs can be flexibly controlled through meso-structural design.

### NTE Metamaterial Categories

NTE metamaterials are classified by deformation mechanism:

**Stretch-Dominant NTE Structures:**
- Achieve NTE effect via axial deformation
- Examples: planar structures based on bi-material triangles, spatial structures based on bi-material tetrahedra, cylindrical shell structures
- Advantages: high relative stiffness
- Limitations: limited regulation range of CTE

**Bending-Dominant NTE Structures:**
- Achieve NTE effect via bending of bi-material straight or curved beams when heated
- Examples: chiral structures, anti-chiral structures, re-entrant structures
- These are essentially auxetic materials with bi-material beams replacing single-material ligaments
- Advantages: high flexibility, wide adjustment range of CTEs, can simultaneously achieve NTE and negative Poisson's ratio (NPR)

### Engineering Applications

Thermal stress impacts various engineering fields significantly:
- **Aerospace**: Thermal stress from ambient temperature changes affects structural safety
- **Precision instruments**: Temperature changes compromise instrument accuracy

Micro-thermal expansion materials and bi-material beams are widely utilized in the design of micro-thermal expansion structures and exhibit great application potential for:
- Manufacturing intelligent sensors
- Realizing structural multi-functional integration
- Mitigating thermal stress effects

### Manufacturing and Validation

With advances in fabrication methods (particularly 3D printing and additive manufacturing), experimental validation of metamaterial properties has become feasible, promoting rapid development in this research field.
