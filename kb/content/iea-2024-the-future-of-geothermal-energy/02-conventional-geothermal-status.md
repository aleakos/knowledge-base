# Conventional Geothermal: Current Status

## Total Energy Use (2023)

- **5 exajoules (EJ)** of geothermal energy consumed globally
- ~0.8% of global total energy demand
- Second least-used clean energy source (after ocean energy)
- Clean energy share context: bioenergy ~7%, hydro/nuclear/wind/solar each 1–3%

**Consumption breakdown:**
- Heat: ~79% (district heating, ground-source heat pumps, industrial)
- Electricity: ~21%

## Top Consuming Countries

The 10 largest consumers account for nearly 90% of global total:
China, United States, Türkiye, Sweden, Indonesia, Iceland, Japan, New Zealand, Germany, Philippines.

- **Iceland**: Nearly half its total final energy consumption from geothermal (policy support since 1920s)
- **China**: ~50% of global geothermal heat consumption (space heating only)
- **United States**: Second largest consumer, primarily electricity
- **Türkiye**: Split between electricity and agriculture/tourism heating

## Electricity Generation

- First geothermal electricity: Italy, 1904 (Larderello 1, 250 kW, 1913)
- **Global generation ~100 TWh in 2023** — 0.3% of total electricity, ~1% of renewable electricity
- Annual generation has nearly doubled in past two decades

**Top electricity producers** (2023): US, Indonesia, Türkiye, Philippines, New Zealand (together two-thirds of global total); Iceland, Italy, Kenya, Mexico, Japan (additional 25%)

**Significant geothermal shares in electricity mix**: Kenya, Iceland, El Salvador, New Zealand, Nicaragua, Costa Rica (all >10%)

## Installed Capacity

- **~15 GW** global geothermal power capacity in 2023 — up ~40% over past decade
- Largest additions 2013–2023: Türkiye, Indonesia, Kenya (>75% of new capacity)
- United States has largest absolute installed capacity (mostly built 1980–1995)

## Capacity Factors

- Global geothermal capacity factor: **75–80%** (5-year average 2019–2023)
- Some countries exceed 90%: New Zealand, Iceland, Italy, Ethiopia
- One geothermal plant produces ~5–6× more energy than a solar PV plant of equal rated capacity

## Power Plant Technologies

| Technology | Minimum Fluid Temperature | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dry steam | Above saturation point | Direct steam to turbine |
| Flash steam | >~180°C | Separate steam from water |
| Binary (ORC) | ~95°C+ | Closed-loop Rankine cycle; lower efficiency, higher cost |

## Heat and Cooling Applications

- Geothermal meets ~1% of global building heat demand
- Applications: district heating, greenhouses, aquaculture, agricultural drying, industrial process heat
- Temperature requirements range from ~50°C (heating) to >300°C (paper/metal processing)
- Ground-source heat pumps: operate with near-surface heat (<100 m depth), provide heating and cooling at <200°C
