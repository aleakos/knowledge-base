# Materials Selection: Carbon AS4/PEKK

## Composite Structure Overview

Composite materials consist of reinforcement fibers embedded in a polymer matrix. The fiber type, orientation, and matrix type govern the resulting mechanical performance. For aerospace applications with large deformation requirements, both components must be selected carefully.

## Reinforcement Fibers

### Aramid (Para-Aramid) Fibers
- High specific strength and stiffness, good vibration damping.
- Poor compressive strength; not suitable for dominant compressive loading.

### Glass Fibers
- Low cost, moderate specific stiffness.
- Typical tensile strength: 1.5–4.8 GPa; modulus: 70–87 GPa.

### Carbon (PAN-Based) Fibers — Selected Material
Carbon fibers are manufactured from polyacrylonitrile (PAN) precursor, structured with turbostratic graphite planes aligned along the fiber axis.

Key properties of selected **Carbon AS4** fiber:
- High specific strength and modulus.
- Better fatigue resistance than glass or aramid.
- Wide range of grades available from standard to ultra-high modulus.

| Property | Range |
|---|---|
| Tensile Strength | 3.5–7.0 GPa |
| Tensile Modulus | 230–900 GPa |
| Failure Strain | 0.3–2.4% |
| Density | 1.75–2.0 g/cm³ |

## Matrix Selection

### Thermosets vs. Thermoplastics

| Feature | Thermoset | Thermoplastic |
|---|---|---|
| Processing | One-way cure | Melt and reprocess |
| Toughness | Lower | Higher |
| Failure strain | Low | Higher (~1%) |
| Recyclability | No | Yes |

Thermoplastics were chosen because their higher failure strain and toughness are essential for large deformation applications.

### PolyEtherKetoneKetone (PEKK)

PEKK belongs to the PAEK (poly aryl ether ketone) family, alongside PEEK. Key advantages:
- Glass transition temperature ~160°C (higher than PEEK).
- Melt processing temperature ~370–400°C.
- Excellent chemical resistance, low moisture absorption.
- High in-plane shear strength; supports energy absorption through matrix deformation.

## Carbon AS4/PEKK Composite Properties

The composite lamina properties (from manufacturer data and experimental characterization):

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Longitudinal modulus E₁ | ~134 GPa |
| Transverse modulus E₂ | ~9 GPa |
| Shear modulus G₁₂ | ~5 GPa |
| Longitudinal tensile strength | ~2070 MPa |
| Transverse tensile strength | ~83 MPa |
| Failure strain (fiber direction) | ~1.5% |

The relatively high failure strain in the ±45° and low-angle configurations makes AS4/PEKK suitable for large-deformation tube designs.

## Fabrication Processes

### Compression Molding
- Prepreg plies are stacked in a steel mold; pressure ~0.7 MPa applied at ~380°C.
- Suitable for flat laminates and test specimens.
- Time-temperature profile: heat to 380°C, hold, cool under pressure.

### Automated Fiber Placement (AFP)
- In-situ consolidation using a heated roller/torch at the NRC-AMTC facility.
- Enables curved and variable-thickness tube geometries.
- Temperature and pressure must be tightly controlled to ensure good consolidation and low void content.
