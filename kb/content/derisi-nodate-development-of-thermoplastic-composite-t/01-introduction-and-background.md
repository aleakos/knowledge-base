# Introduction and Background

## Research Context

This PhD thesis (Concordia University, 2008) addresses the development of thermoplastic composite tubes capable of large deformation, motivated by the need to replace aluminum helicopter landing gear with a composite equivalent that matches the same strength, stiffness, and failure strain as aluminum 7075-T6.

The research was conducted in collaboration with Bell Helicopter, Bombardier, NRC-AMTC, and Ecole de Technologie Superieure under two CRIAQ projects. The material selected was Carbon AS4/PEKK (PolyEtherKetoneKetone), a high-performance thermoplastic composite.

## Helicopter Landing Gear Design Requirements

Helicopter skid landing gear consists of:
- **Cross tubes**: curved tubes of varying wall thickness (16 mm to 3 mm) that absorb energy through plastic deformation during hard landings.
- **Skid tubes**: longitudinal tubes providing lateral stability and suppressing rolling motions.

Key design requirements are contradictory in nature:
- Stress levels must be high enough for energy absorption via plastic deformation.
- Stress levels must remain low enough to prevent fatigue failure.

## Challenges with Aluminum Landing Gear

1. **Fabrication cost**: Varying wall thickness is achieved via acid-etching (chemical milling), a process that is environmentally restricted and increasingly expensive. Fewer companies can meet regulatory requirements.

2. **Corrosion**: Spot corrosion — especially internal — is a primary failure source in service. Corroded regions create stress concentrations that lead to crack propagation under normal loading.

3. **Fatigue**: Aluminum has no endurance limit; failure under fluctuating stress is inevitable over time. Shot-peening and cold-worked holes are used to retard crack growth, but even minor surface scratches increase corrosion-fatigue risk.

4. **Residual strain accumulation**: The yield strain of aluminum is typically below 0.7%. After repeated landings, permanent deformation accumulates, potentially requiring structural replacement and increasing helicopter operating costs.

## Research Objectives

The specific goals of the research were:
- Design a composite tube matching the strength, stiffness, and deformability of the aluminum DN 80, SCH 80 pipe (7075-T6 alloy).
- Demonstrate feasibility of composite landing gear for subsequent implementation into curved cross-tube geometry.
- Introduce Strain Controlled Design as a new methodology for composite structures requiring large deformation.

## Research Plan

1. Review and select thermoplastic composite material (Carbon AS4/PEKK).
2. Characterize mechanical properties of flat plate AS4/PEKK laminates.
3. Review analytical techniques for composite tube stress analysis.
4. Design, manufacture, and test composite tubes — progressively improving the layup through multiple iterations.
5. Validate using ANSYS progressive failure analysis and experimental bending tests.
