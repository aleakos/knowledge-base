# Composite Structures and Material Selection

## Categories of Composites

### Metal Matrix Composites (MMCs)
- Aluminum (2.7 g/cm³) and titanium (4.5 g/cm³) are preferred metals
- Common reinforcements: SiC, Al₂O₃, TiB₂ (whiskers and particulates)
- High cost limits use to applications where no monolithic alternative can meet requirements
- Does not solve corrosion or fabrication challenges of aluminum landing gear

### Ceramic Matrix Composites (CMCs)
- High hardness, wear resistance, and temperature capability
- Catastrophic brittle fracture; no plasticity under load
- Eliminated from consideration due to low deformability and toughness

### Polymer Matrix Composites (PMCs)
- Low density, good chemical resistance, relatively low cost
- Properties limited by service temperature (must remain below glass transition temperature Tg)
- Selected as the material class for landing gear development

**Matrix types within PMCs:**
- *Thermoplastic*: melt on heating, resolidify on cooling — reversible. Linear or branched-chain molecular structure. Can be reshaped with heat and pressure. Examples: PEEK, PEKK, PPS, nylons.
- *Thermosetting*: irreversible cross-linked network formed during cure. Lower processing cost, better fiber impregnation. Examples: epoxies, polyesters, phenolics.

## Fiber Reinforcements

| Fiber Type | Modulus (GPa) | Tensile Strength (MPa) | Elongation (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kevlar 29 | 68 | 2930 | 3.6 |
| Kevlar 49 | 124 | 3700 | 2.8 |
| Kevlar 149 | 186 | 3440 | 2.5 |
| E-Glass | 72 | 1500–3000 | 1.8–3.2 |
| S2-Glass | 86 | 4000 | 5.4 |

**Aramid fibers** (Kevlar): High tensile strength but poor compression and transverse strength due to weak hydrogen bonds. Low longitudinal shear modulus.

**Glass fibers**: Isotropic, low cost, high fracture strain, good moisture resistance. Lower stiffness (72 GPa) than carbon.

**Carbon fibers**: Most important for aerospace. Produced from PAN or pitch precursors under high temperature and tension. High strength-to-weight ratio, wide range of modulus available.

## Selected Material: Carbon AS4/PEKK

PEKK (PolyEtherKetoneKetone) is a high-performance semicrystalline thermoplastic with:
- High glass transition temperature
- Good processability via compression molding and automated fiber placement
- Reversible melt processing (unlike thermosets)

**Carbon AS4/PEKK composite mechanical properties:**
- Available in prepreg tape and tow forms
- Processing temperature profile required for full consolidation
- Filament winding with in-situ consolidation possible

**Fabrication methods used in this research:**
1. *Compression molding*: Wabash press with custom mold; time-temperature profile tuned to AS4/PEKK
2. *Automated fiber placement (AFP)*: AMTC-NRC machine used for tube manufacturing; allows tailored ply angles and thickness gradients

The material was new to market at project start, requiring a full characterization campaign before design work could proceed.
