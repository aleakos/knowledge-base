# Summary, Conclusions, and Future Work

## Summary

This research developed thermoplastic composite tubes (Carbon AS4/PEKK) capable of matching the strength, stiffness, and failure strain of Aluminum 7075-T6 in three-point bending. The work proceeded from material characterization through analytical modeling to prototype fabrication and testing over four iterative tube designs.

## Key Conclusions

1. **Strain Controlled Design** is a valid new design methodology for composite laminates where large deformation is required. It inverts the traditional stiffness-controlled approach by targeting strain capacity first.

2. **Carbon AS4/PEKK** with medium-angle fiber orientations (±30° to ±45°) and optional resin interlayers can achieve failure strains of 3–4%, approaching the ~11% failure strain of aluminum (with appropriate geometric compensation via cross-section sizing).

3. **The sandwich panel model** provides a reliable simplified analysis of composite tube bending behavior. Results agreed with both ANSYS finite element analysis and experimental data.

4. **Delamination** can serve as an energy absorption mechanism. Designing the layup so that 90° layers fail first releases the constraint on adjacent plies, enabling continued load carrying after initial fracture.

5. **Four-point and three-point bending tests** confirmed that composite tubes with aluminum liners (fourth tube) maintained load levels throughout the test — a significant improvement over earlier designs.

## Research Contributions

- First systematic characterization of Carbon AS4/PEKK composite laminates for structural design
- Development and validation of Strain Controlled Design method
- Simplified sandwich panel model for composite tube bending analysis
- Demonstrated feasibility of thermoplastic composite tubes for large-deformation helicopter landing gear application

## Design Process Summary

1. Define target load case (harsh landing bending scenario)
2. Identify required failure strain from worst-case cross section
3. Select layup using Strain Controlled Design (target fiber angles ±30° to ±45°)
4. Size cross section for required stiffness
5. Perform CLT and ANSYS verification
6. Prototype and test

## Future Work

- Implementation of the design into a curved composite tube (actual landing gear geometry)
- Testing under cyclic (fatigue) loading
- Optimization of layup for weight reduction
- Investigation of other thermoplastic resin systems as PEKK alternatives
- Scaling manufacturing to AFP process for production-intent curved tubes
