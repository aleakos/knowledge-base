# Mechanical Characterization of Carbon AS4/PEKK

## Testing Program Overview

Chapter 3 documents a systematic experimental program to characterize the mechanical behavior of Carbon AS4/PEKK laminates needed for tube design. Because the material was relatively new and lacked a reliable database, test specimens were fabricated via compression molding and tested in tension on an MTS testing machine.

Laminate configurations tested:
- 0° (fiber direction)
- 90° (transverse direction)
- [0/45/90/-45]s — quasi-isotropic
- [±45°]₂s
- [±30°]₂s
- [30°/Resin/-30°/Resin]₂s
- [±30°/±45°]s
- [±25°]₂s
- [±20°]₂s

## Specimen Preparation and Quality Control

Preforms were compression molded at ~380°C, 0.7 MPa. Micrographic inspection revealed important quality issues:
- High processing pressure can fracture fibers.
- Resin-rich zones can form if consolidation is inadequate.
- Void content must be minimized to achieve representative mechanical properties.
- Polishing and micrography were used to verify laminate quality before mechanical testing.

## Key Results by Layup

### 0° (Fiber Direction)
- Highest strength and stiffness of all orientations.
- Tensile strength ~2070 MPa, failure strain ~1.5%.
- Failure mode: catastrophic fiber fracture, brittle and sudden.
- Poisson's ratio varies slightly with axial strain.

### 90° (Transverse Direction)
- Very low strength; dominated by matrix and fiber-matrix interface.
- Tensile strength ~83 MPa, very low failure strain (<0.5%).
- Failure by crack propagation parallel to fibers and debonding.

### Quasi-Isotropic [0/45/90/-45]s
- Moderate strength; balanced in-plane properties.
- Damage progression visible before final fracture.

### ±45° Laminates
- High shear strain capability; large deformation before failure.
- Failure strain significantly greater than 0° or 90°.
- Fibers rotate during tensile loading, creating geometric nonlinearity.

### ±30° Laminates
- Intermediate between ±45° and 0° behavior.
- Specimens show fiber rotation during test; this contributes to apparent large deformation.
- Fracture occurs progressively.

### [30°/Resin/-30°/Resin]₂s
- Resin interlayers act as crack arrestors.
- Failure strain increased compared to [±30°]₂s without resin layers.
- Delamination between fiber-rich and resin-rich layers is a primary failure mode.

### ±25° and ±20° Laminates
- As angle decreases toward 0°, behavior transitions toward fiber-dominated (brittle) fracture.
- ±20° laminates show limited deformation capacity.

## Summary: Failure Strain vs. Fiber Angle

A key finding is that **medium-angle laminates (±25° to ±45°) achieve the highest failure strains** and are most suitable for large-deformation tube designs. Low-angle laminates (0°, ±20°) are stronger but more brittle.

| Layup | Approx. Failure Strain |
|---|---|
| 0° | ~1.5% |
| 90° | <0.5% |
| [±45°]₂s | >3% |
| [±30°]₂s | ~2–3% |
| [±25°]₂s | ~2% |
| [±20°]₂s | ~1.5% |

This database informed the Strain Controlled Design procedure developed in Chapter 5.
